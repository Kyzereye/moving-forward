# Statement of Desire: Perfect Health

*Based on Chapter 2 (Desire) of Napoleon Hill's *Think and Grow Rich*. Updated as I read the book.*

---

## My Initial Question

Using the concepts on desire, chapter 2 in Napoleon Hill's book Think and Grow Rich, I want to put together a few statements of desire.

The first is for perfect health. I desire perfect hearing, eyesight, mental acuity, ease of motion, attractive looks, strong lean muscles. The ability to eat whatever I want and still maintain an attractive physic. What else?

---

## Response: Expanding the Health Desires

Hill's formula (the Six Steps) usually focuses on money, but it adapts to physical transformation. To make the statement truly "Hill-esque," add **vitality, endurance, and cellular intelligence.**

### Additional high-level concepts to round out the list

* **Immune Resilience:** The ability to move through the world without fear of seasonal illness or environmental stressors.
* **Restorative Sleep:** Waking up every morning with a "surplus of energy" (a favorite Hill phrase) and feeling completely rejuvenated.
* **Longevity/Vitality:** Not just looking good, but having the internal "biological age" of someone much younger.
* **Subconscious Autonomic Function:** Having your body automatically regulate its weight, digestion, and repair systems without constant conscious effort.

---

## Applying the 6 Steps to Health

| Step | How to Apply it to Health |
| --- | --- |
| **1. Be Definite** | Instead of "perfect health," define your ideal weight or a specific physical feat (e.g., "I weigh 180 lbs with 10% body fat"). |
| **2. The Sacrifice** | What will you give in return? (e.g., "In return, I give my body disciplined movement and mindful choices.") |
| **3. The Date** | When will you achieve this state? Set a specific deadline. |
| **4. The Plan** | Create a definite plan for exercise and nutrition and **start at once.** |
| **5. The Script** | Write it out clearly. |
| **6. The Ritual** | Read it aloud twice daily—once before bed and once upon waking. |

> My metabolism is so efficient that it easily converts nourishment into energy, maintaining my attractive physique regardless of my environment. In return for these blessings, I will treat my body with the respect it deserves, following my plan of [Name your exercise/habit] and maintaining a mindset of gratitude.
> I believe I have this health in my possession now. My subconscious mind is currently rebuilding every cell to match this blueprint."

### Reality check (from response)

Hill would likely suggest that the "eat whatever I want" goal is better framed as a desire for a **metabolism so high and a lifestyle so active** that the body handles fuel like a high-performance engine—less about "magic" and more about the body becoming a master processor of energy.
