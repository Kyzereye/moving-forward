# Mental & Learning Plan

*Part of the Definite Plan for the Health Desire Statement. Reading 30 min daily; learning banjo; exploring many interests.*

---

## Core habits

- **Reading:** 30 minutes per day (book of choice; can tie to interests below).
- **Banjo:** Regular practice; concentration/focus to be decided as you go.

---

## Your interest areas (starting list)

| Area | Ideas to start learning more |
| --- | --- |
| **Physics** | One popular-science book (e.g. *A Brief History of Time*, *Seven Brief Lessons*); short YouTube series (e.g. PBS Space Time, Veritasium); one “concept per week” from a list you keep. |
| **Banjo** | Structured course or method book (e.g. Earl Scruggs, or an online program); 10–15 min daily drills + one tune per week; listen to players you like and copy phrases. |
| **Interesting people** | Biographies or memoirs from one interest area at a time; podcasts (e.g. long-form interviews); one “person per month” you read or listen about. |
| **Poker** | One book on strategy (e.g. *The Theory of Poker* or a beginner text); play low-stakes or play-money to apply; review one hand or concept per week. |
| **Chess** | Free app (e.g. Lichess, Chess.com); do tactics 10 min/day; one opening for white, one for black; watch one game or lesson per week. |
| **Law of attraction / self-development** | Re-read *Think and Grow Rich* and note one idea per chapter; one other book (e.g. *The Power of Your Subconscious Mind*, *Atomic Habits*); practice one technique (e.g. desire statement, visualization) daily. |
| **Ocean & diving** | Documentary or book on marine life or diving; plan one dive trip or skill (e.g. certification, new site); follow one ocean-science or diving channel. |
| **Weather** | One intro book or online course on weather; check one forecast source and try to predict next day; follow a meteorology blog or YouTube. |
| **Gardening** | One book or channel on your zone and soil; one crop or project per season; keep a short “what worked” log. |
| **Birds** | Local field guide or app (e.g. Merlin); 10 min observation or listing per week; one “bird of the week” to learn. |
| **Outer space** | One book (e.g. *Cosmos*, *Astrophysics for People in a Hurry*); follow one space mission or telescope; one concept per month (e.g. black holes, exoplanets). |
| **Stock market** | One intro book (e.g. *A Random Walk*, *The Little Book of Common Sense Investing*); paper trade or small real account; follow one index or sector. |
| **Software & programming** | One language or track (e.g. Python, web dev); one small project (automation, script, or tiny app); 30 min coding on set days. |
| **Travel** | One destination per season to research; list of “must-see” and “must-do”; tie reading or documentaries to that place. |
| **Photography** | One course or book on basics (exposure, composition); one theme (e.g. birds, travel, daily objects); weekly photo goal (e.g. 5 keepers). |

## How to use this file

- Pick **1–3** areas to focus on at a time (e.g. banjo + reading + one other).
- Use the “Ideas to start” column as a checklist; add your own resources and notes below.
- Rotate: when one topic feels routine, swap in another from the list.
- Revisit when you find your other “burning desire” notes and tie them into this plan.
