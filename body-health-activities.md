---

## Activity ideas by category

### Strength (gym, 2x/week)
- Full-body or upper/lower split.
- Focus on: compound lifts (squat, hinge, push, pull), then 1–2 accessories if time.
- Keep sessions 45–60 min so they’re sustainable.

### Cardio (2x/week – not necessarily “standard”)
- **Outdoor:** Bike (trail or mountain), hike in the mountains, long walk on a trail.
- **Sport:** Pickleball (counts as cardio + fun).
- **Indoor (optional):** Treadmill, rower, elliptical, or stair climber if weather or schedule demands it.
- Mix by week: e.g. one hike, one pickleball; or one bike, one hike.

### Daily walk (30 min)
- Same time each day (e.g. morning or after work) to make it automatic.
- Can be neighborhood, park, or part of a trail; can merge with errands or a podcast/audiobook.

### Yoga (3–4x/week)
- **At home:** Follow a video or a short sequence (e.g. 20–30 min) on non-gym or light days.
- **Class (optional):** One or two studio/online classes per week for structure.
- Spread across the week so it supports recovery and flexibility without overloading any single day.

## Example week (one way to arrange it)

- **Mon:** 30 min walk, yoga.
- **Tue:** Gym strength, 30 min walk.
- **Wed:** Cardio (e.g. hike or pickleball), 30 min walk, yoga.
- **Thu:** Gym strength, 30 min walk.
- **Fri:** 30 min walk, yoga.
- **Sat:** Cardio (e.g. bike or pickleball), 30 min walk.
- **Sun:** 30 min walk, yoga (or rest if 3 sessions already done).

---

## Notes for evolution

- Start with the minimum that feels doable (e.g. 2 strength, 2 cardio, daily walk, 3 yoga), then add the 4th yoga or extra cardio as it becomes habit.
- If a week is busy, protect: daily walk + 2 strength; treat cardio and yoga as “fill in as possible.”
- Revisit this outline when life or goals change (e.g. more hiking in summer, more indoor in winter).
