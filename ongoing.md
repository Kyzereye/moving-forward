# Ongoing Desires

*Long-term desires that are not tied to a single deadline. They unfold over time and stay active. Structured using the Desire chapter from Napoleon Hill's *Think and Grow Rich*.*

---

## 1. Money

- **When:** Immediately.
- **Desire:** More money than I know what to do with pours into my existence quickly and very easily. I give most of it away. It is my desire to be a millionaire.
- **What I give in return:** I provide excellent service for whatever I am receiving the money for. I am also a conduit for honest people and efforts to receive the money they need.

### Statement to read and focus on twice daily

> Begining immediately, more money than I know what to do with pours into my existence quickly and very easily. I greatly enjoy being a multi-millionaire. In return, I give a lot of it away to worthy causes and I enjoy being a conduit for honest people and efforts to receive the money they need. 

### Ways to focus on the feeling of having it

- **Sensory snapshot (30–60 seconds).** After reading the statement, close your eyes and drop into one moment: the ease of knowing money is flowing in, the freedom of giving a lot of it away, the calm of that this kind of money can bring. Feel your body relaxed and open. Notice what you feel. Do this right after each reading.

- **"One perfect moment" scene.** Pick one scene you can replay (e.g. writing a check to someone who needs it, receiving payment for excellent service, feeling the relief of "enough"). Run through it in your mind with as much sensory detail as you like. Focus on the emotion—ease, freedom, generosity, peace. Replay that same scene each time.

- **One-word feeling.** Choose one word for how you want to feel with this money (e.g. *ease*, *free*, *generous*, *peace*, *flow*). After reading the statement, say the word and feel it in your body for 10–20 seconds.

- **Contrast then shift.** Notice how you feel *without* it—the weight of scarcity or worry. Don't dwell. Then switch: "And now it is flowing in. I have more than I need." Let the feeling change—relief, ease, openness. The shift is the practice.

- **Gratitude-as-if.** After the statement, add 2–3 sentences in present tense: "I am grateful for the money that flows to me. I am grateful that I can give most of it away. I am grateful to be a conduit for others." Speak as if it's already true and sit with the feeling.

- **Physical anchor.** Pick one gesture (e.g. hands open, palms up; or hand on heart). When you do the twice-daily read, use that gesture and pair it with the feeling of money flowing in and flowing out. Over time the gesture can trigger the feeling quickly.

Use one option every time, rotate a few, or combine (e.g. read statement → one-word feeling → 30-second sensory snapshot).

## 2. Life is easy / everything flows

- **When:** Immediately.
- **Desire:** Life is very easy for me. Everything flows. I meet life and people with curiosity. Everyone is working to help me, and I quickly realize this and grasp the patterns of life and people—who they are and why they have entered my life. I meet awesome people who are trusted friends; they support me and help me to be a better person.
- **What I give in return:** I meet life and people with curiosity. I help other people in kind. I am part of the flow.

### Statement to read and focus on twice daily

> Beginning immediately, life is very easy, fun, and adventureous for me. Everything flows easily. I meet life and people with curiosity. Everyone is working to help me, and I quickly realize this and grasp the patterns of life and people. I meet awesome people who are trusted friends.  They support me and help me to be a better person. In return, I graps and understand who they are and why they have entered my life. I help other people in kind. I am part of the flow.

### Ways to focus on the feeling of having it

- **Sensory snapshot (30–60 seconds).** After reading the statement, close your eyes and drop into one moment: life easy, fun, and adventurous; everything flowing; curiosity about the people around you; the clarity of grasping who someone is and why they're in your life; trusted friends supporting you and helping you be a better person. Feel your body relaxed and open. Notice what you feel. Do this right after each reading.

- **"One perfect moment" scene.** Pick one scene you can replay (e.g. a fun or adventurous moment when everything flowed, or the instant you grasped why someone had entered your life, or a trusted friend supporting you or helping you grow, or you helping someone in kind and feeling part of the flow). Run through it in your mind with as much sensory detail as you like. Focus on the emotion—ease, fun, curiosity, clarity, connection, supported. Replay that same scene each time.

- **One-word feeling.** Choose one word for how you want to feel (e.g. *ease*, *flow*, *fun*, *curious*, *clear*, *connected*, *supported*, *adventurous*). After reading the statement, say the word and feel it in your body for 10–20 seconds.

- **Contrast then shift.** Notice how you feel when life feels hard, dull, or opaque—when you don't see how people fit or feel unsupported. Don't dwell. Then switch: "And now life is easy, fun, and adventurous. Everything flows. I have trusted friends who support me and help me be a better person. I grasp who they are and why they're in my life." Let the feeling change—relief, ease, curiosity, openness. The shift is the practice.

- **Gratitude-as-if.** After the statement, add 2–3 sentences in present tense: "I am grateful that life is easy, fun, and adventurous. I am grateful for the awesome people who are trusted friends—they support me and help me be a better person. I am grateful to help others in kind and be part of the flow." Speak as if it's already true and sit with the feeling.

- **Physical anchor.** Pick one gesture (e.g. hands open, palms up; or a slight nod). When you do the twice-daily read, use that gesture and pair it with the feeling of ease, flow, fun, curiosity, and being supported by trusted friends. Over time the gesture can trigger the feeling quickly.

Use one option every time, rotate a few, or combine (e.g. read statement → one-word feeling → 30-second sensory snapshot).
