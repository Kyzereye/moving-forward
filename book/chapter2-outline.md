# Chapter 2 outline: What moving forward means

*Scope: This chapter defines what “moving forward” means—nothing else. No deep dive into why we get stuck, habits, goals/desire frameworks, or step-by-step how-to (those belong in other chapters).*

*Sources woven in: your draft in `02-what-moving-forward-means.md`; your project-management framing; ideas from [Why and How to Keep Moving Forward?](https://medium.com/illumination/why-and-how-to-keep-moving-forward-8c7c11f68e57) (Infinitians, ILLUMINATION)—used only where they clarify **meaning**, not the article’s full “why” or “how” sections.*

---

## 1. Open with the half-finished life (keep / tighten)

- Half-finished projects as a concrete image: not about clutter as clutter, but about **attention and energy left behind**.
- What “not moving forward” looks like in ordinary spaces (garage, shelf, corner of a room)—same idea as draft, possibly shorter setup.

---

## 2. Core definition: forward is where attention and the next action go

- **Not** replaying failure, **not** waiting for perfect conditions or a past version of yourself.
- **Is** putting attention on **what comes next** instead of what already happened or what “it should have been.”
- You don’t have to erase the past; you have to **stop living inside the story of it** while deciding what to do now.
- **Whole route optional; next step required** (aligns with “stop looking at the rearview, look at the road”).
- **Energy / momentum:** harder to start from standstill than to keep moving; stale work feels heavier over time—**one small step** breaks the freeze (from your draft).

---

## 3. “Moving forward” in the sense of a project or plan

*Your principles, spelled out as part of the definition:*

- Moving forward with a project means **taking action** to **advance**, **continue**, or **implement** the plan—not staying indefinitely in preparation mode.
- Often means **shifting from planning to execution**: doing the thing the plan was for, not only refining the plan.
- Implies **driving progress**: observable motion toward the next phase or milestone, not just intention.
- Includes **working through or around obstacles** without treating a stall as the final state.
- Includes **keeping tasks and commitments to the work** aligned so the effort doesn’t scatter.
- Aims at **completion or a deliberate stopping point**—forward motion has a direction relative to “done” or “next phase,” not endless drift.

*Additional principles (same chapter, still definitional):*

- **Forward vs. busywork:** rearranging the garage or re-planning can *feel* like progress; forward means the **project itself** moves (matches your woodworking story).
- **Forward vs. perpetual restart:** always chasing the newer, shinier idea can mean **never advancing** any single line of work—still a definition contrast, not a lecture on discipline.
- **Pace:** forward can be **steady walking**, not sprinting; the Medium piece’s line that you don’t have to run or stop—you **keep walking**—fits here as *what forward is*, not a productivity hack.

---

## 4. Two valid shapes of “forward” (keep both stories)

- **Forward by finishing:** one rule, one focus, small steps until something is **done**—your woodworking arc.
- **Forward by releasing:** **dropping** what no longer matches the life you’re building; **keeping the learning**, **releasing the obligation**—degree story.
- State clearly: both are forward; **neither** is pretending the past didn’t happen.

---

## 5. Vision vs. plan (from Medium, as meaning only)

- **Moving forward** is **not** the same as **never changing tactics**. If the plan fails, **changing the plan** can still be forward—as long as you’re not confusing **giving up the vision** with **adjusting the method** (“change the plan, not the vision” in the article’s terms).
- Contrast for definition only: **hammering one failed tactic forever** isn’t forward; **trying another way toward the same honest aim** is. (Avoid turning this into a full strategy chapter—one tight paragraph or short subsection.)

---

## 6. Direction that is yours

- **Forward** implies a **front**—a way that is **yours**, not only a script written by someone else (echo Chapter 1 without re-telling it).
- Brief: if “forward” is only other people’s markers, you may be **moving** without **advancing your own line**—still definitional, not a sermon on passion.

---

## 7. Close with the reader (keep DNA of current ending)

- **This week**, not “in general” or “someday.”
- One step on a stalled project **or** one honest release **or** one shift from plan-only to one execution step.
- **What did the stall teach you?** Keep the lesson; you’re not required to keep the weight.
- **Question:** where does your mind go—**back** or **ahead**? Then one small choice that matches **ahead**.

## What not to put in this chapter (reminder)

- Why we ruminate (save for “why we get stuck”).
- Full habit systems, identity work, desire/goal frameworks (other chapters).
- Long motivational “why the world needs you to keep going” (the Medium piece’s “you are not alone” / inspiration chain—powerful, but not **definition**).
- Step-by-step “how to keep moving” playbooks (Medium’s “listen to your heart,” “screw the results” sections can inform **later** chapters if you want; here, at most a **single sentence** if it defines forward, e.g. forward is **engaging the work you control**, not freezing on the scoreboard).
